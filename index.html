Action and Reaction

Public Scrutiny
& Advocacy

When you actually do the work instead of just posting about it, you occasionally make people uncomfortable. My advocacy has drawn significant scrutiny regarding my stance as an Anti Zionist Jewish American.

I grew up in a proud Jewish household in Dayton, attending Hillel Academy of Dayton and Oakwood High School. My perspective shifted in 2015 after traveling to the occupied West Bank and witnessing segregated settlements firsthand. Living in Dearborn Michigan surrounded by the largest Arab American population in the country deepened that understanding further.

What followed was a highly publicized sequence of events that tested exactly how much institutional discomfort the community was willing to tolerate.

Act I

The Stand

I stood up for Palestinian rights by speaking at a Call It Genocide rally in downtown Dayton. The speech immediately drew criticism and was covered extensively by the Dayton Jewish Observer.

Act II

The First Press Release

In response to the coverage, I put out a formal press release commending the paper for publishing an Anti Zionist Jewish perspective. I explicitly pointed out that public opinion is shifting rapidly and that communal institutions need to reflect that changing reality.

Act III

The Backlash and Second Criticism

That statement caused an uproar. I was doxxed by a far right extremist group and received a flood of bomb threats and death threats directed at me and my family. Then, I was criticized again in the Dayton Jewish Observer. The President of the local Jewish Federation published a dedicated op ed acting in direct response to my press release, explicitly rejecting my stance and defending the institutional status quo.

Act IV

The Krakow Response

I did not back down. I put out another press release directly responding to the Federation. I wrote it from Krakow, Poland, where I was visiting Holocaust memorial sites. My message was incredibly simple. Never Again is not conditional. It means never again for anyone.
